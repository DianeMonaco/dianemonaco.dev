Shin Megami Tensei III: Nocturne

Shin Megami Tensei III: Nocturne is an RPG released in Japan in 2003. It is an entry in the mainline Shin Megami Tensei series, a series of games taking place in a post-apocalyptic Tokyo. The player takes the role of a high school student who finds himself turned into a half-demon and thrown into an alternate world where demons and deities rule. The only humans left are two of his classmates, his teacher, a reporter, and a mysterious man who orchestrated the apocalypse.

The game's narrative explores themes of helplessness, drive to power, and isolation in an increasingly technologically connected world. The remaining humans are given the opportunity by a mysterious cosmic will to reshape the world as they see fit and create their own personal utopia. Each character struggles with their own insecurities and ambitions in their journey to create the world they wish to see, recruiting like-minded demons in their ambition to re-create the world.

Of course, the demons and deities in the narrative mirror the desires of the humans who created them. Some seek a world where might makes right and only the strong are fit to survive. Others envision a world where everyone is completely isolated from the pains associated with knowing other people. Some desire a world of complete silence where every living being in the universe can seek personal enlightenment away from any distractions.

There is no clear answer as to which path is the correct one, if any. The player must choose for themselves what they think is right, be it accepting one of the three paths presented to them or rejecting all of them and fighting against the powers that put them in this position in the first place. While the player is free to choose from the paths presented to them, they are forbidden from forging their own path: despite having the ultimate authority on how the world is reshaped, they are powerless to create their own vision. Throughout the story, the player becomes isolated from the people that they once counted as friends. Even when the player is given a choice that seems meaningful, they are ultimately powerless.

Everything in the game's narrative serves to explore the themes of powerlessness and isolation. The closer the end is, the further away the player drifts from the people they once knew. Whether or not the player chooses to reject or embrace their humanity, moving on means losing people they once loved. Oh, and the combat system is super fun.
